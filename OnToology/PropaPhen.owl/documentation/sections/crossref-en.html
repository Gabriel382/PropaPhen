Observation of Propagation Phenomenon Occurrencec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/def/propaphen#Observation

As a propagation phenomenon is still a general phenomenon (" Smith, David Woodruff. « Phenomenology ». The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, édité par Edward N. Zalta, Summer 2018, Metaphysics Research Lab, Stanford University, 2018. Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, https://plato.stanford.edu/archives/sum2018/entries/phenomenology/. "), different points of view or observations may result in different experiences. A phen:Observation is a gufo:Perdurant that represents a spatial-temporal perspective of a phen:PhenomenonOccurrence (i.e., two observations from different people may differ in the number of details one can give). In practice, a phen:Observation may be used to identify what gufo:Individual are participating in a phen:PhenomenonOccurrence for a given node of the network for a given time interval. As phen:Observation designs a spatial-temporal cluster itself, it can be used to track valid spatial-temporal paths between several pairs of phen:Observation.

Phenomenon Occurrencec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/def/propaphen#PhenomenonOccurrence

A gufo:Perdurant that represents the occurrence of a phen:Phenomenon. Similar to the concept of the occurrence of information being propagated in the social information propagation phenomenon on "Król, Dariusz, Damien Fay, et Bogdan Gabryś, éd. Propagation Phenomena in Real World Networks. Vol. 85. Intelligent Systems Reference Library. Cham: Springer International Publishing, 2015. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-15916-4.". Another example would be an outburst in a network from a particular virus type. For example, the occurrence of a hurricane in Paris is different from the occurrence of a hurricane in the United States if they are happening simultaneously. Therefore, they are two different instances of phen:PhenomenonOccurrence of the same phen:Phenomenon (Hurricane).

Propagation Phenomenon Typec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/def/propaphen#Phenomenon

It is based on the meaning of the Propagation Phenomenon presented by "Król, Dariusz, Damien Fay, et Bogdan Gabryś, éd. Propagation Phenomena in Real World Networks. Vol. 85. Intelligent Systems Reference Library. Cham: Springer International Publishing, 2015. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-15916-4." A phenomenon is a gufo:Perdurant that defines a fact or event of scientific interest susceptible to scientific description and explanation (Merriam-Webster dictionary). It does not define the occurrence of a phenomenon but the study of a type of it (i.e., Epidemic phenomena such as COVID-19, Influenza, etc.). As the mentioned phenomena, the study of a phenomenon may only be valid from the first occurrence of it until the last occurrence of it.

Has Spatial-Temporal Valid Pathop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/def/propaphen#hasSTValidPath

Represents a valid spatial-temporal path between two observations. I.e. From the same phen:PhenomenonOccurrence "Hurricane in France on 2019", we may have different phen:Observations like "Hurricane observed in Rouen on November,2019" and "Hurricane observed in Paris on December,2019". We may say that there is a possibility that the Hurricane went from Rouen to Paris, so there is a valid spatial-temporal path between the first and the second observation.

has characteristics: asymmetric

has domain
Observation of Propagation Phenomenon Occurrence c
has range
Observation of Propagation Phenomenon Occurrence c
